Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

I am passionate about education and bridging the gap between artificial intelligence research and renewable energy applications. My teaching philosophy centers on making complex AI concepts accessible through real-world renewable energy examples, fostering critical thinking about sustainable technology solutions, and preparing students to tackle climate challenges through intelligent systems.

Courses

IKT215: Pattern Recognition

Comprehensive course covering pattern recognition techniques with emphasis on applications in renewable energy systems. Students learn classification algorithms, feature extraction, and deep learning methods through hands-on projects involving wind turbine data analysis and energy system optimization.

Teaching Approach

My approach to teaching is grounded in the belief that learning should be an active, engaging process. I strive to create an environment where students feel comfortable asking questions, making mistakes, and exploring ideas.

Active Learning

Encouraging hands-on experience through projects and interactive discussions.

Collaborative Environment

Fostering peer learning and teamwork in problem-solving.

Real-world Applications

Connecting theoretical concepts to practical, industry-relevant problems.
