About Me

I am a PhD Research Fellow and Assistant Professor at the University of Agder, Norway, specializing in artificial intelligence applications for renewable energy systems and wind farm analytics. With a unique interdisciplinary background spanning electrical engineering, renewable energy, and AI, I focus on developing innovative solutions for the sustainable energy sector.


I am passionate about bridging the gap between cutting-edge artificial intelligence research and real-world applications that accelerate our transition to sustainable energy systems. My work focuses on developing intelligent solutions that not only advance scientific knowledge but also contribute meaningfully to addressing climate challenges and creating a more sustainable future for generations to come.


Currently, I'm a key researcher in the AIMWind project, a collaborative initiative between the CAIR and the Mechatronics Group at UiA. My research centers on machine learning and analytics for wind farm life extension, performance optimization, and predictive maintenance.

Research Overview

My research spans multiple areas of computer science, with a particular focus on:

  • Machine Learning and Deep Learning
  • Data Science and Analytics
  • Application of AI in Renewable Energy
  • Condition monitoring and predictive maintenance for wind energy systems
  • Wind resource analysis and wind farm optimization
  • Renewable energy grid integration and microgrid design
