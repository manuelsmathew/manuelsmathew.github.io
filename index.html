About Me

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Agder (UiA), specializing in the intersection of artificial intelligence and renewable energy systems. Originally from Kerala, India, my academic journey has taken me across continents in pursuit of innovative solutions for sustainable energy.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Mahatma Gandhi University in India, and my first Master's degree in Systems Engineering by Research from the University of Brunei Darussalam (UBD). During my time at UBD, I was affiliated with the UBD | IBM Center, an industry-academic collaboration focusing on AI applications in power system management.

I joined the University of Agder in 2019 as a Master's student in Renewable Energy, where I completed my thesis on applications of AI in integrated renewable energy systems. Currently, I am pursuing my PhD as part of the AIMWind (Analytics for Asset Integrity Management of Wind Farms) project, a collaboration between the Center for Artificial Intelligence Research (CAIR) and the Mechatronics Group at UiA.

My research focuses on developing machine learning and analytical approaches to extend the operational life of wind farms, contributing to more sustainable and efficient renewable energy infrastructure.
