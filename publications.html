Publications

My research focuses on artificial intelligence applications for renewable energy systems, with publications in leading conferences and journals covering wind energy analytics, turbine diagnostics, and sustainable energy optimization. Below is a selection of my recent publications organized by year.

2024

"Enhanced Diagnostics for Wind Turbine Pitch System Using Deep Learning"

Manuel S. Mathew, Dimitrios Tzivaras, Knut E. Giljarhus

European Conference of the Prognostics and Health Management Society (PHME 2024)

This paper introduces a novel algorithm to detect and localize faults in induction motors within wind turbine pitch systems using SCADA data. Our deep learning approach significantly improves fault detection accuracy and enables proactive maintenance strategies.

PDF Code arXiv

"Wind Farm Performance Estimation using Deep Learning Architectures"

Manuel S. Mathew, Knut E. Giljarhus, Dimitrios Tzivaras

European Conference of the Prognostics and Health Management Society (PHME 2024)

We present a comprehensive deep learning architecture for estimating wind farm-level performance using SCADA data. This research enables more accurate performance monitoring and optimization strategies for large-scale wind energy installations.

PDF Code arXiv

2023

"Systems Perspective on Public Perception of Wind Power in Norway"

Manuel S. Mathew, Torgeir Moan, Knut E. Giljarhus

International Council on Systems Engineering Symposium (INCOSE IS 2024)

This research applies systems thinking methodologies to understand the formation of public perception regarding wind power projects in Norway. We provide insights into stakeholder dynamics and decision-making processes affecting renewable energy deployment.

PDF DOI

"Machine Learning Approaches for Renewable Energy System Performance Modeling"

Manuel S. Mathew, Knut E. Giljarhus

International Conference on Smart Energy Systems and Technologies (SEST 2022)

We present machine learning methodologies for modeling the performance of renewable energy systems, with specific applications to wind and solar energy installations. Our approach enables better prediction and optimization of renewable energy output.
